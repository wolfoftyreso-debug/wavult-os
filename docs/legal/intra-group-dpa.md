## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Controller is the ultimate parent and group data controller for the Wavult Group and determines the purposes and means of processing personal data on behalf of the Group;

**WHEREAS**, the Processor processes personal data of EU data subjects (including Zoomers domiciled in the EU) on behalf of the Controller in connection with the operation of the QuiXzoom platform in the European Economic Area;

**WHEREAS**, the transfer of personal data from the Processor (located in the EU/EEA) to the Controller (located in the United Arab Emirates, a third country without an EU adequacy decision) requires appropriate safeguards pursuant to Chapter V of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (the **"GDPR"**);

**WHEREAS**, the Parties wish to execute this Data Processing Agreement in accordance with Article 28 GDPR and incorporate the Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021 (**"SCC Decision"**) as the appropriate safeguard for the restricted transfer;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, the Parties agree as follows:

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1** **"GDPR"** means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data.

**1.2** **"Personal Data"** has the meaning given in Article 4(1) GDPR, and specifically includes the categories of Zoomer Data described in Clause 3.

**1.3** **"Processing"** has the meaning given in Article 4(2) GDPR.

**1.4** **"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is processed under this Agreement, including Zoomers domiciled in the EU/EEA.

**1.5** **"Sub-processor"** means any processor engaged by the Processor who processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller in connection with this Agreement.

**1.6** **"Supervisory Authority"** means the competent data protection authority, including the Lithuanian State Data Protection Inspectorate (*Valstybinė duomenų apsaugos inspekcija*, **"VDAI"**) and any other relevant EU member state authority.

**1.7** **"Restricted Transfer"** means a transfer of Personal Data from the Processor (EU) to the Controller (UAE), which constitutes a transfer to a third country within the meaning of Chapter V GDPR.

**1.8** **"SCCs"** means the Standard Contractual Clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2016/679, as adopted by Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021.

**1.9** **"Platform"** means the QuiXzoom mobile application, web application, and all associated technology infrastructure operated by QuiXzoom UAB and other Wavult Group entities.

**1.10** **"Security Incident"** means any breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed.

## 2. ROLE OF THE PARTIES

**2.1 Controller.** Wavult Group FZCO acts as the **Data Controller** for the Wavult Group. The Controller determines the purposes and means of processing Zoomer Data across all Group entities.

**2.2 Processor.** QuiXzoom UAB acts as a **Data Processor** on behalf of the Controller for Personal Data of EU/EEA-domiciled data subjects processed in connection with the Platform's EU operations.

**2.3 Independent Controller.** To the extent that QuiXzoom UAB processes Personal Data for its own independent purposes (e.g., compliance with Lithuanian law), it acts as an independent controller for such processing.

**2.4 Other Group Entities.** Other Wavult Group entities (QuiXzoom Inc., Landvex AB, Landvex Inc., Wavult DevOps FZCO) may act as Sub-processors or independent controllers as separately documented.

## 3. CATEGORIES OF PERSONAL DATA AND DATA SUBJECTS

**3.1 Data Subjects.** The Personal Data processed under this Agreement relates to the following categories of data subjects:

  (a) Zoomers (independent contractors) registered on the Platform and domiciled in the EU/EEA;  
  (b) Potential Zoomers in the onboarding process; and  
  (c) Any other EU/EEA natural persons whose Personal Data is collected by the Platform in connection with Zoomer operations.

**3.2 Categories of Personal Data.** The categories of Personal Data processed include:

| Category | Examples | Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|
| Identification data | Full name, date of birth, identity document number | Standard |
| Contact data | Email address, phone number, postal address | Standard |
| Financial data | Bank account number (IBAN/SWIFT), payment history, earnings records | Standard (sensitive in context) |
| Location data | GPS coordinates, location history captured during Missions | Standard (sensitive in context) |
| Device data | Device identifiers, IP addresses, platform usage logs | Standard |
| Visual/media data | Photographs and video content captured and uploaded by Zoomers | Standard |
| Tax/business data | Tax identification numbers, VAT numbers, business registration details | Standard |
| Verification data | Identity verification documents, selfies submitted for KYC | Standard (sensitive in context) |

**3.3 Special Category Data.** The Parties do not intend to process special category data (Article 9 GDPR) under this Agreement. In the event that any Content uploaded by a Zoomer inadvertently contains special category data (e.g., images revealing health conditions, religious symbols, or political affiliation of third parties), the Processor shall implement automated and manual review processes to detect and segregate such data and shall notify the Controller immediately.

## 4. PURPOSES AND LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING

**4.1 Purposes.** Personal Data is processed under this Agreement for the following purposes:

  (a) **Platform operation:** Account management, Mission assignment and management, Deliverable review and acceptance;  
  (b) **Payment processing:** Calculation, verification, and disbursement of Compensation to Zoomers;  
  (c) **Quality control:** Review and assessment of Zoomer performance and Deliverable quality;  
  (d) **Compliance:** Tax compliance, identity verification (KYC/AML), fraud prevention;  
  (e) **Customer support:** Handling Zoomer queries and disputes; and  
  (f) **Security and platform integrity:** Detection and prevention of fraudulent, abusive, or unlawful use.

**4.2 Legal Basis.** The Controller confirms that processing is based on one or more of the following legal bases under Article 6 GDPR:

  (a) **Article 6(1)(b) GDPR** — performance of the contract with the Zoomer;  
  (b) **Article 6(1)(c) GDPR** — compliance with legal obligations (tax, AML, labor law);  
  (c) **Article 6(1)(f) GDPR** — legitimate interests of the Controller (fraud prevention, platform security).

## 5. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

**5.1 Instructions.** The Processor shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, unless required to do so by EU or member state law applicable to the Processor. In such cases, the Processor shall inform the Controller of the legal requirement before processing, unless prohibited from doing so.

**5.2 Confidentiality.** The Processor shall ensure that persons authorized to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

**5.3 Security Measures.** The Processor shall implement the technical and organizational security measures specified in Clause 7.

**5.4 Sub-processing.** The Processor shall not engage any Sub-processor without prior specific or general written authorization of the Controller. The Processor shall maintain a list of authorized Sub-processors and shall notify the Controller of any intended changes with at least thirty (30) days' prior notice, giving the Controller the opportunity to object. The Processor shall impose data protection obligations equivalent to those in this Agreement on any Sub-processor.

**5.5 Data Subject Rights.** The Processor shall, taking into account the nature of processing, assist the Controller with its obligation to respond to requests for the exercise of Data Subjects' rights under Chapter III GDPR (Clauses 8 and 9 below).

**5.6 Data Protection Impact Assessments.** The Processor shall assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with Articles 32–36 GDPR (security, notification of breaches, DPIAs, prior consultation) taking into account the nature of processing and information available.

**5.7 Return and Deletion.** At the choice of the Controller, the Processor shall delete or return all Personal Data to the Controller after the end of the provision of processing services, and delete existing copies unless EU or member state law requires retention of the Personal Data.

**5.8 Audit Rights.** The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this Agreement and shall allow for and contribute to audits and inspections conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, with reasonable notice.

## 6. RESTRICTED TRANSFER AND STANDARD CONTRACTUAL CLAUSES

**6.1 Third Country Transfer.** Personal Data processed by the Processor (EU) will be transferred to and further processed by the Controller (UAE). The UAE does not benefit from an EU adequacy decision under Article 45 GDPR.

**6.2 Applicable SCCs.** The transfer of Personal Data from the Processor to the Controller shall be governed by the Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 4: Processor-to-Controller) as set out in Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914, which are incorporated into this Agreement by reference and shall be deemed executed by both Parties.

**6.3 Module 4 — Processor to Controller.** The Parties confirm the following selections under Module 4 of the SCCs:

  (a) **Clause 7 (Docking Clause):** Applicable — additional group entities may accede with mutual agreement;  
  (b) **Clause 11 (Redress):** No independent redress body designated;  
  (c) **Clause 17 (Governing Law):** Lithuanian law shall govern the SCCs (as the law of the EU member state in which the Processor is established);  
  (d) **Clause 18 (Choice of Forum):** Lithuanian courts shall have jurisdiction for disputes relating to the SCCs.

**6.4 Supplementary Measures.** In accordance with the European Data Protection Board's Recommendations 01/2020 on Supplementary Measures, the Parties shall implement the following supplementary technical and organizational measures:

  (a) **Encryption in transit:** All Personal Data transferred from the EU to the UAE shall be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher;  
  (b) **Encryption at rest:** Personal Data stored in UAE infrastructure shall be encrypted at rest using AES-256;  
  (c) **Access restriction:** Access to Personal Data in the UAE shall be limited to personnel with a documented operational need;  
  (d) **Pseudonymization:** Where technically feasible, Personal Data shall be pseudonymized before cross-border transfer.

**6.5 Transfer Impact Assessment.** The Parties have conducted a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) for the transfer to the UAE and have determined that, taking into account the nature of the data, the technical measures in Clause 6.4, and the legal framework of the UAE (including Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data — **"PDPL"**), the supplementary measures provide an essentially equivalent level of protection. A copy of the TIA shall be maintained and made available to the relevant Supervisory Authority upon request.

**6.6 UAE PDPL Compliance.** The Controller acknowledges its obligations under the UAE PDPL and shall ensure that processing in the UAE complies with the PDPL alongside the obligations assumed under the SCCs.

## 7. TECHNICAL AND ORGANIZATIONAL SECURITY MEASURES

**7.1** The Processor (and, by extension, the Controller for processing in UAE) shall implement and maintain the following technical and organizational measures:

**7.2 Technical Measures:**

  (a) **Encryption:** All Personal Data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256);  
  (b) **Access Control:** Role-based access control (RBAC) limiting data access to authorized personnel only; multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all administrative access;  
  (c) **Pseudonymization:** Zoomer IDs and location data pseudonymized in analytics and reporting systems;  
  (d) **Network Security:** Firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and regular penetration testing;  
  (e) **Logging and Monitoring:** Audit logs of all access to Personal Data, retained for a minimum of 12 months;  
  (f) **Backup and Recovery:** Regular encrypted backups; tested business continuity and disaster recovery plan.

**7.3 Organizational Measures:**

  (a) Privacy training for all staff with access to Personal Data;  
  (b) Designation of a Data Protection Officer (DPO) or data protection responsible person in the EU (QuiXzoom UAB);  
  (c) Documented data protection policies, procedures, and records of processing activities (RoPA) per Article 30 GDPR;  
  (d) Regular internal data protection audits;  
  (e) Vendor due diligence and DPA requirements for all Sub-processors.

## 8. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

**8.1** The Parties shall cooperate to enable Data Subjects to exercise their rights under GDPR Chapter III, including:

  (a) **Right of Access (Article 15 GDPR):** The Processor shall extract and provide Personal Data held about a Data Subject within five (5) business days of a request from the Controller;

  (b) **Right to Erasure (Article 17 GDPR):** The Processor shall delete (not merely anonymize) the relevant Data Subject's Personal Data within fifteen (15) business days of a documented instruction from the Controller, subject to Clause 9 (Retention) and any legal retention obligations;

  (c) **Right to Data Portability (Article 20 GDPR):** The Processor shall provide Personal Data in machine-readable format (JSON or CSV) within fifteen (15) business days of a request from the Controller;

  (d) **Right to Rectification (Article 16 GDPR):** The Processor shall correct inaccurate Personal Data promptly upon instruction from the Controller;

  (e) **Right to Restriction (Article 18 GDPR):** The Processor shall restrict processing where requested by the Controller in accordance with Article 18 GDPR;

  (f) **Right to Object (Article 21 GDPR):** The Processor shall cease processing based on legitimate interests upon instruction from the Controller where a valid Article 21 objection has been received.

**8.2** The Processor shall notify the Controller within three (3) business days of receiving any direct Data Subject rights request and shall not respond to such requests directly without prior instruction from the Controller.

---

## 9. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

**9.1 Retention Period.** Personal Data of Zoomers shall be retained for a period of **three (3) years** from the date of the Zoomer's last completed Mission on the Platform (the **"Retention Period"**).

**9.2 Extended Retention.** Personal Data may be retained beyond the Retention Period only where:

  (a) required by applicable law (e.g., tax retention requirements under Swedish Bookkeeping Act (*Bokföringslagen*), Lithuanian tax law, or US tax regulations);  
  (b) necessary for ongoing legal proceedings or regulatory investigations; or  
  (c) subject to a documented legal hold instruction from the Controller.

**9.3 Deletion Process.** Upon expiry of the Retention Period:

  (a) all Personal Data shall be permanently and irreversibly deleted from all production systems, databases, and backups;  
  (b) deletion shall be documented and a deletion certificate provided to the Controller upon request;  
  (c) Sub-processors shall be instructed to delete their copies in accordance with their own DPAs.

**9.4 Anonymization.** Where anonymization (as defined by GDPR Recital 26) is sufficient for the Controller's purposes, anonymized data may be retained beyond the Retention Period.

---

## 10. SECURITY INCIDENT NOTIFICATION

**10.1 Internal Notification.** The Processor shall notify the Controller of any Security Incident without undue delay and in any event **within 24 hours** of becoming aware of the incident, providing all available information sufficient to enable the Controller to fulfill its notification obligations.

**10.2 Regulatory Notification.** The Controller shall notify the relevant Supervisory Authority (and, where required, the VDAI as the Processor's lead supervisory authority) of a Security Incident **within 72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach, in accordance with Article 33 GDPR.

**10.3 Data Subject Notification.** Where a Security Incident is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects (Article 34 GDPR), the Controller shall notify affected Data Subjects without undue delay, with the Processor's assistance.

**10.4 Incident Content.** Notifications pursuant to Clause 10.1 shall include, where available:

  (a) a description of the nature of the incident;  
  (b) the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected;  
  (c) the categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected;  
  (d) the likely consequences of the incident; and  
  (e) measures taken or proposed to address the incident.

**10.5 Incident Register.** The Processor shall maintain a Security Incident register documenting all incidents (including near-misses) and the measures taken in response.

**11.2** The RoPA shall be made available to the relevant Supervisory Authority upon request.

## 12. DATA PROTECTION OFFICER

**12.1** QuiXzoom UAB shall appoint or designate a Data Protection Officer (DPO) or equivalent data protection responsible person in the EU to oversee compliance with this Agreement and GDPR.

**12.2** The DPO's contact details shall be published on the Platform's Privacy Policy page.

## 13. LIABILITY

**13.1 Indemnification.** Each Party shall indemnify the other against claims, damages, fines, and costs (including legal fees) arising from that Party's failure to comply with its obligations under this Agreement or applicable data protection law.

**13.2 Regulatory Fines.** The Parties acknowledge that breaches of GDPR may result in administrative fines of up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover under Article 83 GDPR. Each Party shall be liable for fines attributable to their own actions or omissions.

---

## 14. TERM AND TERMINATION

**14.1 Term.** This Agreement shall remain in force for as long as the Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller in connection with the Platform.

**14.2 Termination.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing sixty (60) days' written notice.

**14.3 Post-Termination.** Upon termination, the Processor shall, at the Controller's election, delete or return all Personal Data and certify deletion in writing, unless required to retain data under applicable law.

---

**15.1** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the Republic of Lithuania, consistent with the selection in the SCCs (Clause 6.3(c)).

**15.2** Any dispute arising from this Agreement shall be submitted to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Lithuanian courts.

**15.3** The SCCs incorporated under Clause 6.2 are governed by Lithuanian law and subject to Lithuanian court jurisdiction as specified therein.
