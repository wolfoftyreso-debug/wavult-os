## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

**1.1 Purpose.** This Transfer Pricing Policy (the **"Policy"**) establishes the framework for pricing intercompany transactions within the Wavult Group. The Policy is designed to:

  (a) ensure that all intercompany transactions are priced on arm's length terms consistent with the OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises and Tax Administrations (the **"OECD Guidelines"**);  
  (b) comply with the transfer pricing documentation requirements in each jurisdiction in which the Group operates;  
  (c) minimize the risk of transfer pricing adjustments and associated penalties; and  
  (d) support the Group's overall business and tax strategy.

**1.2 Scope.** This Policy applies to all intercompany transactions between entities within the Wavult Group, including but not limited to:

  (a) IP licensing and royalty arrangements;  
  (b) management services fees;  
  (c) intragroup financing; and  
  (d) provision of shared services.

**1.3 Legal Basis.** This Policy is grounded in Article 9 of the OECD Model Tax Convention on Income and Capital, which establishes the arm's length principle as the international standard for pricing transactions between associated enterprises.

---

## 2. THE ARM'S LENGTH PRINCIPLE

**2.1 OECD Standard.** All intercompany transactions shall be priced as if they had been carried out between independent, unrelated parties operating under comparable circumstances and at arm's length. This principle derives from Article 9(1) of the OECD Model Tax Convention, which provides:

> *"[W]here conditions are made or imposed between the two enterprises in their commercial or financial relations which differ from those which would be made between independent enterprises, then any profits which would, but for those conditions, have accrued to one of the enterprises, but, by reason of those conditions, have not so accrued, may be included in the profits of that enterprise and taxed accordingly."*

**2.2 Functional Analysis.** For each intercompany transaction type, a functional analysis shall be conducted assessing:

  (a) **Functions performed** by each party (including employees' activities);  
  (b) **Assets used** (including intangible assets and IP);  
  (c) **Risks assumed** (market, financial, operational, and regulatory risks).

## 3. TRANSFER PRICING METHODS

**3.1 Primary Method — Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP).** The Group adopts the Comparable Uncontrolled Price method as the primary transfer pricing methodology, consistent with OECD Guidelines Chapter II, Part A. Under the CUP method, the intercompany price is compared to the price charged in comparable transactions between independent parties under comparable circumstances.

**3.2 Application of CUP.** When applying the CUP method:

  (a) Internal CUPs (transactions between a Group entity and an unrelated third party) shall be preferred where available;  
  (b) External CUPs (transactions between two unrelated parties in the market) shall be used where internal CUPs are not available;  
  (c) Comparability adjustments shall be made for material differences in product, contractual terms, economic conditions, and business strategies.

**3.3 Secondary Methods.** Where the CUP method cannot be reliably applied, the following methods may be used in order of preference:

  (a) Resale Price Method (RPM);  
  (b) Cost Plus Method (C+);  
  (c) Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM);  
  (d) Profit Split Method (PSM) — only where other methods cannot be reliably applied.

**3.4 Benchmarking.** Transfer prices shall be benchmarked against arm's length ranges derived from commercially available databases (e.g., Bureau van Dijk Orbis, Bureau van Dijk Amadeus, or equivalent), to be updated at each Annual Review (Clause 7).

## 4. IP LICENSING ARRANGEMENTS

**4.1 Licensor.** Wavult DevOps FZCO (Dubai) is the registered owner and principal developer of all Group intellectual property, including software platforms, algorithms, datasets, trademarks, and trade secrets pertaining to the QuiXzoom and Landvex platforms (collectively, the **"Group IP"**).

**4.2 License Fee — Rate.** Each operating subsidiary that utilizes the Group IP shall pay a royalty fee to Wavult DevOps FZCO equivalent to **ten percent (10%) of the subsidiary's Net Revenue** in respect of each financial year (the **"IP License Fee"**).

**4.3 Definition of Net Revenue.** For the purposes of this Policy, **"Net Revenue"** means the total gross revenue of the relevant subsidiary for the relevant financial year, less:

  (a) refunds and chargebacks;  
  (b) applicable sales taxes, VAT, and similar consumption taxes collected for and remitted to tax authorities; and  
  (c) payment processing fees directly attributable to platform transactions.

**4.4 Payment Terms.** IP License Fees shall be invoiced quarterly in arrears by Wavult DevOps FZCO to each operating subsidiary and shall be payable within thirty (30) days of the invoice date.

**4.5 Currency.** Unless otherwise agreed in writing, all intercompany invoices shall be denominated in US Dollars (USD). Currency conversion shall be performed at the average monthly exchange rate published by the European Central Bank (ECB) or equivalent authoritative source for the relevant month.

**4.6 Arm's Length Justification.** A royalty rate of 10% of net revenue is consistent with arm's length rates observed for:

  (a) software-as-a-service (SaaS) platform licenses in the technology sector;  
  (b) crowdsourcing platform intellectual property licenses;  
  (c) comparable data collection and analytics platform IP arrangements.

This rate shall be reviewed and benchmarked annually in accordance with Clause 7.

## 5. MANAGEMENT FEES

**5.1 Service Provider.** Wavult Group FZCO (Dubai) provides group-level management, strategic oversight, governance, treasury, legal coordination, and centralized administrative services to operating subsidiaries (the **"Management Services"**).

**5.2 Management Fee — Rate.** Each operating subsidiary shall pay Wavult Group FZCO a management services fee equivalent to **five percent (5%) of the subsidiary's Net Revenue** in respect of each financial year (the **"Management Fee"**).

**5.3 Services Covered.** The Management Fee covers the following services:

  (a) Group strategy, leadership, and governance;  
  (b) Treasury and cash management oversight;  
  (c) Legal and regulatory coordination;  
  (d) Brand and intellectual property governance;  
  (e) HR policy framework and senior recruitment coordination;  
  (f) IT infrastructure oversight and cybersecurity governance; and  
  (g) Consolidated financial reporting and investor relations.

**5.4 Service Level Agreement.** A separate intragroup Service Level Agreement (SLA) shall be maintained between Wavult Group FZCO and each operating subsidiary documenting the specific services rendered and service standards applicable.

**5.5 Payment Terms.** Management Fees shall be invoiced quarterly in arrears and payable within thirty (30) days.

**5.6 Arm's Length Justification.** A management fee of 5% of net revenue is consistent with market rates for centralized management services in technology holding structures, as observed in published transfer pricing databases and comparable arrangements.

## 6. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS BY JURISDICTION

**6.1 General Principle.** Each Group entity shall maintain contemporaneous transfer pricing documentation sufficient to demonstrate that its intercompany transactions comply with the arm's length principle. Documentation must be prepared in advance of the filing of the entity's tax return.

---

### 6.2 Sweden (Landvex AB)

**Applicable Law:** Swedish Income Tax Act (*Inkomstskattelagen*, IL) Chapter 14, Sections 19–20; and the Swedish Tax Agency's (*Skatteverket*) regulations on transfer pricing documentation (SKVFS 2007:1, updated by SKVFS 2019:2).

**Requirements:**

  (a) Written transfer pricing documentation (*internprissättningsdokumentation*) must be prepared by Landvex AB for each intercompany transaction above de minimis thresholds;  
  (b) Documentation shall describe the business, the transactions, the pricing method, and the comparability analysis;  
  (c) Documentation must be available to the Swedish Tax Agency (*Skatteverket*) within 30 days of request;  
  (d) Documentation shall be prepared in Swedish or English (both accepted by Skatteverket);  
  (e) Landvex AB must maintain a Country-by-Country Report (CbCR) readiness as part of the Group's obligations if consolidated group revenue exceeds SEK 7 billion (otherwise CbCR obligations rest with the Parent in UAE); and  
  (f) Failure to maintain adequate documentation may result in penalties up to 100% of the income adjustment under Chapter 49 IL.

---

### 6.3 Lithuania (QuiXzoom UAB)

**Applicable Law:** Lithuanian Law on Corporate Income Tax (*Pelno mokesčio įstatymas*, PMĮ), Article 40; Lithuanian Transfer Pricing Regulations (State Tax Inspectorate Order No. VA-35, 2004, as amended); OECD Guidelines as incorporated by reference.

**Requirements:**

  (a) QuiXzoom UAB must maintain a Master File and Local File in accordance with the Lithuanian regulations implementing BEPS Action 13;  
  (b) The Local File must cover all transactions with related parties exceeding EUR 90,000 in the aggregate per year;  
  (c) Documentation must be submitted to the State Tax Inspectorate (*Valstybinė mokesčių inspekcija*, VMI) upon request;  
  (d) Documentation may be in English; a Lithuanian translation may be required upon request;  
  (e) Transfer pricing adjustments may result in corporate income tax at 15% plus interest and penalties.

---

### 6.4 United States (QuiXzoom Inc. and Landvex Inc.)

**Applicable Law:** Internal Revenue Code Section 482; Treasury Regulations §1.482-1 through §1.482-9; IRS Revenue Procedure 2006-9 (APA procedures).

**Requirements:**

  (a) QuiXzoom Inc. and Landvex Inc. must maintain Section 482 contemporaneous documentation prior to the filing of each US federal tax return;  
  (b) Documentation must include a functional analysis, industry analysis, economic analysis, description of the method selected and alternatives rejected, and supporting benchmarking data;  
  (c) Adequate contemporaneous documentation reduces the risk of a 20% (or 40% for gross valuation misstatements) transfer pricing penalty under Section 6662;  
  (d) Transfer pricing documentation shall be maintained for a minimum of seven (7) years;  
  (e) If cumulative intercompany transactions with a single counterparty exceed USD 10 million in a year, enhanced documentation obligations apply;  
  (f) Consideration of Advance Pricing Agreement (APA) with the IRS shall be reviewed at each Annual Review if the US entities' revenues materially increase.

---

### 6.5 United Arab Emirates (Wavult Group FZCO and Wavult DevOps FZCO)

**Applicable Law:** UAE Corporate Tax Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022); UAE Transfer Pricing Guidelines (Ministerial Decision No. 97 of 2023); OECD Guidelines as adopted by the UAE Federal Tax Authority (FTA).

**Requirements:**

  (a) Both UAE entities must comply with UAE transfer pricing documentation requirements effective from financial years commencing on or after 1 June 2023;  
  (b) A Master File and Local File must be maintained where total revenues exceed AED 200 million or where the entity is part of a multinational group required to file a CbCR;  
  (c) Transfer pricing disclosures must be submitted with the annual corporate tax return;  
  (d) Transactions with UAE free zone entities and transactions between free zone and mainland entities shall be carefully documented;  
  (e) Note: Free Zone entities may qualify for a 0% corporate tax rate on qualifying income — this qualification must not be jeopardized by non-arm's length intercompany transactions.

## 7. ANNUAL REVIEW

**7.1 Frequency.** This Policy shall be reviewed annually, no later than ninety (90) days after the end of each financial year (the **"Annual Review"**).

**7.2 Scope of Annual Review.** The Annual Review shall include:

  (a) Benchmarking of IP License Fee rates against updated comparable data;  
  (b) Benchmarking of Management Fee rates against updated comparable data;  
  (c) Review of functional analysis and risk allocation across the Group;  
  (d) Assessment of any changes in applicable law or OECD Guidelines;  
  (e) Assessment of any significant changes in the Group's business model, functions, or revenues;  
  (f) Update of all Local Files and Master File; and  
  (g) Sign-off by CFO and CEO (Clause 9).

**7.3 Policy Updates.** Material changes to this Policy shall be documented in a revised Policy version, re-approved by the CEO and CFO, and distributed to all Group entities.

---

## 8. INTERCOMPANY AGREEMENTS

**8.1** All intercompany transactions covered by this Policy must be supported by written intercompany agreements that:

  (a) are signed prior to or contemporaneously with the commencement of the transaction;  
  (b) accurately reflect the arm's length terms determined under this Policy; and  
  (c) are maintained in the Group's legal documentation repository.

**8.2** As a minimum, the following intragroup agreements shall be in place:

  (a) IP License Agreement — Wavult DevOps FZCO → each operating subsidiary;  
  (b) Management Services Agreement — Wavult Group FZCO → each operating subsidiary;  
  (c) Intra-group Data Processing Agreement (see separate DPA);  
  (d) Cash Pooling or Intercompany Loan Agreement(s), if applicable.
